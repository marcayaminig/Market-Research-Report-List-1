<p><p>The electric vehicle battery recycling market encompasses several battery types, each with unique characteristics. Nickel–cadmium batteries have high cycle durability but contain toxic materials. Nickel–metal hydride batteries offer better energy density and are less toxic, while lithium-ion batteries dominate EVs due to their efficiency and lightweight design. Lithium polymer batteries are similar to lithium-ion but offer flexible packaging and enhanced safety. Lead-acid cells, though heavier and less efficient, are cost-effective and widely used in traditional vehicles, presenting recycling challenges and opportunities.</p></p>
