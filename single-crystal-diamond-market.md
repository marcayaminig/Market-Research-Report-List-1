<p><p>The single crystal diamond market is primarily divided into two types: HPHT (High Pressure, High Temperature) and CVD (Chemical Vapor Deposition). HPHT diamonds are synthesized by mimicking the natural conditions of diamond formation, resulting in high-quality gems often used in industrial applications. In contrast, CVD diamonds are created by chemical reactions that deposit carbon atoms onto a substrate, allowing for precise control over characteristics and purity, making them popular for both industrial uses and gemstones.</p></p>
