<p><p>Allergy shots, or immunotherapy, are categorized into two main types: sublingual immunotherapy (SLIT) and subcutaneous immunotherapy (SCIT). SLIT involves placing allergens under the tongue for gradual desensitization, offering a more convenient, at-home option, particularly for those with mild to moderate allergies. SCIT, on the other hand, consists of injections administered in a clinical setting, typically more effective for severe allergies. Additionally, "Others" may include alternative therapies or emerging treatments in immunotherapy that seek to enhance patient outcomes.</p></p>
