<p><p>The water-based polyurethane adhesive market can be categorized into three primary types: anionic, cationic, and others. Anionic adhesives contain negatively charged ions, enhancing their stability and bonding capabilities, particularly with polar substrates. Cationic adhesives, conversely, utilize positively charged ions, allowing for strong adhesion on various surfaces and improved resistance to moisture and heat. The "others" category encompasses different formulations that combine features of both types or include other additives, catering to specific industrial applications and performance requirements.</p></p>
